About Me

As a computational physicist and data scientist, I thrive on combining the power of numerical simulations with data-driven insights to unravel the material properties and optimize industrial applications. With a storng theoritical background in physics, statistics and machine learning, I possess the ability to combine interdisciplinary approaches to tackle complex problems in material research.

As a Postdoctoral researcher, I am involved in combining atomistic simulations with data analysis and machine learning algorithms to model high temperature dynamics of glasses under irradation by femtosecond lasers. My research extends to understanding cyrstallization of glasses under laser heating involving use of various clustering algorithm to segregate crystal-like atoms from non-crystal like atoms using atomic environments as features.

During my PhD research, I was involved in writing various molecular dynamics and Markov Chain Monte Carlo codes using Python programming language. Majority of my time was devoted to the implementation and optimization of an in-house simulation package termed Force Enhanced Atomic Refinement (FEAR) that involves combining Reverse Monte Carlo refinement with force-field driven Conjugate gradient energy minimization to create realistic models of materials. Furthermore, I gained a hands-on experience of using data analysis to study the X-ray and neutron diffraction data on materials obtained from high energy synchrotron experiment at different national labaratories.

Performing computer simulations using state-of-the-art simulation softwares (e.g. LAMMPS) required a complete understanding of the source code written in Python thereby pushing me to learn programming languages like Python.Today, I collaborate with researchers across domains, harnessing the power of computer simulations and programming skills to better understand complex phenomena observed in experiments. When I manage some free time, I enjoy learning about current machine-learning and AI advancements. Whether it's exploring material properties or dissecting intricate datasets, I'm dedicated to pushing boundaries and fostering innovation by combining physics and machine-learning.
